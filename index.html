Student Retention Analysis: From Static Tables to Interactive Dashboards

The Problem

Retention reports existed but weren't actionable. Data lived in a 20,000-row spreadsheet with over 30 columns, and visuals were low-resolution screenshots embedded in Word documents. The information was there but reading it required significant effort from the audience.

The Approach

Rather than rebuilding from scratch, I first understood the existing calculation logic — how retention rates were computed, how cohorts were tracked, and how institutional retention differed from program-level retention. From there, I redesigned the visualization iteratively, applying churn analysis principles to an academic context.

The Evolution
Figure 1 & 2 show the final tabular format used in institutional reports — structured and readable, but still static. Figure 3 shows the retention chart redesigned using churn analysis principles, where bars represent retention rates by population and the line tracks total student loss per cohort term.

The final stage, currently in development, moves this into Power BI — enabling dynamic comparison of current performance against historical averages, with filters by student population and campus.

⚠️ Note: All visuals are reference images recreated with synthetic data to preserve institutional confidentiality. This work was independently developed within a post-secondary education context in Canada.

The Result

The same underlying data, presented differently, became a tool for decision-making rather than a reporting obligation.
